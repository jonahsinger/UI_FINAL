Debt Management

Strategies to pay off debt efficiently and save money on interest

Smart Debt Repayment Strategies

Key Concept: How you approach debt repayment can significantly impact the total amount you pay and how quickly you become debt-free. Strategic debt repayment can save you thousands in interest payments.

Consumer debt can be a significant obstacle to financial freedom. Understanding how to efficiently pay off debt is a crucial financial skill that can save you money and reduce financial stress.

Strategic debt management can significantly reduce the total interest paid over time

Important Note

While this lesson focuses on debt repayment strategies, prevention is always better than cure. Try to minimize taking on high-interest debt whenever possible.

Popular Debt Repayment Methods

There are several strategies for tackling multiple debts. Two of the most popular approaches are:

Debt Avalanche

Pay minimum payments on all debts while putting extra money toward the debt with the highest interest rate. This method minimizes the total interest paid.

Debt Snowball

Pay minimum payments on all debts while putting extra money toward the smallest debt first. This method provides psychological wins through early debt elimination.

The Debt Avalanche Method

How the Debt Avalanche Works

  1. List all your debts and their interest rates
  2. Arrange them from highest to lowest interest rate
  3. Make minimum payments on all debts
  4. Put any extra money toward the debt with the highest interest rate
  5. Once the highest-rate debt is paid off, move extra payments to the next highest-rate debt
  6. Repeat until all debts are paid
Advantages of the Avalanche Method
  • Mathematically optimal - saves the most money on interest
  • Reduces the total time to debt freedom (slightly)
  • Logical approach that eliminates the most expensive debt first
Debt Avalanche Example

Credit Card: $3,000 at 22% APR

Pay first

Personal Loan: $5,000 at 12% APR

Pay second

Car Loan: $10,000 at 5% APR

Pay third

Avalanche Benefit

The avalanche method is mathematically optimal, resulting in the lowest total interest paid and typically the fastest path to debt freedom.

The Debt Snowball Method

Debt Snowball Example

Medical Bill: $1,000 at 0% APR

Pay first

Credit Card: $3,000 at 22% APR

Pay second

Car Loan: $10,000 at 5% APR

Pay third

How the Debt Snowball Works

  1. List all your debts and their balances
  2. Arrange them from smallest to largest balance
  3. Make minimum payments on all debts
  4. Put any extra money toward the debt with the smallest balance
  5. Once the smallest debt is paid off, move extra payments to the next smallest debt
  6. Repeat until all debts are paid
Advantages of the Snowball Method
  • Provides quick wins for psychological motivation
  • Reduces the number of separate payments faster
  • Can be more sustainable due to the motivation of seeing debts eliminated
Snowball Benefit

The snowball method can be more motivating as you eliminate individual debts faster, which may lead to better long-term success for some people despite paying slightly more interest.

Which Method Is Right For You?

The avalanche method will save you money on interest, but the snowball method might provide more motivation through quick wins. Choose the approach that best fits your personality and financial situation.
