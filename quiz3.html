What is the main difference between the Debt Avalanche and Debt Snowball methods?

A. Debt Avalanche focuses on paying off the newest debts first.
B. Debt Snowball focuses on paying off the highest interest debts first.
C. Debt Avalanche focuses on highest interest rates, while Snowball focuses on smallest balances first.
D. There is no difference between the two methods.
Correct!

The Debt Avalanche method prioritizes paying off debts with the highest interest rates first to minimize total interest paid, while the Debt Snowball method focuses on paying off the smallest balances first to build momentum and motivation.

Incorrect

The Debt Avalanche method prioritizes debts with the highest interest rates to minimize total interest paid, while the Debt Snowball method targets the smallest balances first to give you quick wins and psychological momentum.

Which debt repayment method will typically save you the most money in interest?

A. Debt Avalanche
B. Debt Snowball
C. Both methods save the same amount
D. Neither method affects interest payments
Correct!

By targeting high-interest debts first, the Debt Avalanche method minimizes the total interest you'll pay over time, making it mathematically optimal for saving money.

Incorrect

The Debt Avalanche method typically saves the most money because it targets high-interest debts first, reducing the total interest paid over the life of your debts.

What is a potential psychological advantage of the Debt Snowball method?

A. It always saves you more money than other methods.
B. It allows you to pay off all debts in one single payment.
C. It provides quick wins by eliminating smaller debts faster, boosting motivation.
D. It requires less total money to implement than other methods.
Correct!

The Debt Snowball method helps provide psychological momentum by giving you the satisfaction of completely paying off smaller debts quickly, which can help maintain motivation throughout your debt repayment journey.

Incorrect

The main psychological advantage of the Debt Snowball method is that it provides quick wins by eliminating smaller debts faster. This creates a sense of progress and accomplishment that keeps many people motivated to continue their debt payoff journey.

What should you do with the minimum payment of a debt after it's completely paid off?

A. Reduce your total monthly payment amount.
B. Put the money into savings immediately.
C. Add it to the payment of your next target debt.
D. Spend it on a reward for yourself.
Correct!

This is the "snowball" or "avalanche" effect - once a debt is paid off, you roll that payment amount into the next debt, creating increasingly larger payments and accelerating your debt payoff.

Incorrect

To maximize the effectiveness of both the Avalanche and Snowball methods, you should roll the freed-up payment amount into your next target debt. This creates the "snowball" effect, with increasingly larger payments that help you eliminate debts faster.

True or False: Making extra payments toward debt principal can significantly reduce the total interest paid over time.

A. True
B. False
Correct!

Extra payments that go directly to principal reduce the balance on which interest is calculated, potentially saving you significant money and shortening your repayment period.

Incorrect

Making extra payments toward principal is one of the most effective ways to reduce total interest paid. Since interest is calculated based on your remaining balance, reducing the principal faster means less interest accumulates over time.
