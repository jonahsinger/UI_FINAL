What is the main goal of diversification in a portfolio?

A. To maximize returns in a single stock.
B. To reduce overall risk by investing in different assets.
C. To avoid paying taxes.
D. To only invest in technology stocks.
Correct!

Diversification spreads your investments across various assets or sectors, reducing the impact of any one underperforming investment.

Incorrect

The main goal of diversification is to reduce overall risk by investing in different assets. This way, if one investment performs poorly, the others may help offset the losses.

Which of the following is an example of a diversified portfolio?

A. Investing all your money in a single gold mining company.
B. Investing in multiple tech companies and ignoring all other sectors.
C. Investing in a mix of stocks from different sectors, plus some bonds.
D. Keeping all your savings in cash only.
Correct!

Spreading funds across different sectors (tech, healthcare, consumer goods, bonds, etc.) is a classic diversification strategy.

Incorrect

A diversified portfolio includes investments across different sectors and asset classes. Investing in a mix of stocks from different sectors, plus some bonds, provides better diversification than concentrating in a single company or sector.

True or False: Diversification guarantees you will never lose money.

A. False
B. True
Correct!

Diversification does not guarantee no losses; it simply helps to mitigate risk. During market-wide downturns, even diversified portfolios may lose value.

Incorrect

Diversification helps reduce risk, but it does not guarantee you will never lose money. In widespread market downturns, even well-diversified portfolios may lose value, though usually less than concentrated portfolios.

If you expect one sector to do exceptionally well, should you put your entire portfolio into that sector?

A. Yes, because it maximizes potential gain.
B. No, because if that sector underperforms, you could face significant losses.
C. It depends on how lucky you feel.
D. There's no difference.
Correct!

Even if you expect big gains, concentrating solely on one sector increases risk significantly. Market predictions are often wrong, and unexpected events can cause sectors to underperform.

Incorrect

While putting everything in one sector might maximize potential returns if your prediction is correct, it also maximizes your risk. If that sector underperforms (which happens frequently, even to experts), you could face significant losses. Diversification provides more balanced outcomes.

Diversification can involve:

A. Different companies, sectors, or even asset classes.
B. Only high-growth stocks.
C. Only dividend-paying stocks.
D. None of the above.
Correct!

Diversification often includes different company sizes, industries, and asset classes like bonds, real estate, or cash-like instruments.

Incorrect

Diversification involves spreading investments across different companies, sectors, and asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.). Focusing only on one type of investment (like high-growth or dividend stocks) doesn't provide true diversification.
